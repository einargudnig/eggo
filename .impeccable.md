## Design Context

### Users
Icelandic businesses of all sizes — from startups to established companies. Many are non-technical decision-makers who need software built but don't speak "developer." They're evaluating whether Eggo is trustworthy and capable enough to hand their project to.

### Brand Personality
**Capable, warm, direct.** Eggo is the team that gets things done without overcomplicating it. No jargon, no posturing — just confidence backed by competence. Think of a skilled craftsperson who explains things plainly and delivers reliably.

### Aesthetic Direction
Clean and refined, not flashy. Warm amber accent (`#d4a054` dark / `#b8863a` light) with stone-tinted neutrals. The design should feel intentional and considered — evidence of taste, not decoration. Avoid generic tech/startup aesthetics (especially emerald green, the default consultancy color). Typography should do the heavy lifting over decorative elements. Instrument Serif for headings, DM Sans for body.

### Design Principles
1. **Let the words work** — The Icelandic copy is strong. Design should amplify it, not compete with it.
2. **Earned confidence** — Show competence through craft (typography, spacing, details) not through decoration (gradients, glows, orbs).
3. **Clarity over cleverness** — Non-technical visitors should feel welcome. No jargon in the UI, no ambiguity in the hierarchy.
4. **Warmth through texture** — Avoid clinical minimalism. Use type weight, spacing rhythm, and subtle warmth to feel human.
5. **Every element earns its place** — If it doesn't communicate or guide, remove it.
